Prediction of a secondary cardiac event

During clinical trials, the response to a treatment is not the same for every patient, it is very difficult to predict the clinical trajectories. To improve the efficacy of the treatment (treatmentâ€™s effect under ideal conditions) and also to reduce the risk of clinical trial failure, identification of patients with high chances of having a disease-related event and targeting patients who are more likely to respond to an intervention or a drug is essential. The number of patients with a cardiovascular event is very low in comparison to the patients without a cardiovascular event and among the patients who had a cardiovascular event, the number of patients experiencing a second event is even lower. To handle this highly imbalanced classification problem, various machine learning models, ensemble models and resampling methods have been explored by making using of the clinical data extracted using ICD-9 and ICD-10 codes.



Low Resource Machine Translation

A low resource setting is when there isn't enough data at hand, to train models. Large language models like transformers have huge number of parameters and in order to avoid over-fitting, the models need large amounts of data to be trained with. For least spoken languages, translating them to another language is difficult because there isn't much data available. To tackle this challenge, a self-learning technique has been explored that utilizes the source-side monolingual data to strengthen the encoder and boost the performance of the transformer model by generating synthetic data.



Solar Irradiance prediction

Solar irradiance nowcasting is to predict reliable short-term forecasts of the solar irradiance that has a direct influence on the solar power generation. The accurate prediction of solar energy available is important for PV energy plants in order to facilitate the planning and operation of photovoltaic systems, which in turn reduce the operation cost of these electric systems. Making using of the GOES-13 satellite imagery, ConvNets are built to predict the solar irradiance values at four different time periods.



User Profiling in Social Media

Knowing about a user's likes and dislikes, search history, reviews/comments can aid in understanding the preferences of the user. Social profiling is constructing a user profile (i.e., infer age, gender, personality, demographics) by leveraging textual (messages, reviews, comments), visual (photos) and relational cues (likes and dislikes) from social media. This project involves training models to predict age, gender and Big 5 personality types. User profiling helps in making decisions and recommendations to the users based on their activity on the social media.
